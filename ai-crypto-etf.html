✦ The Investment Thesis
What Would It Take to Launch This ETF?
The regulatory and market conditions required for each layer to become a real product
LAYER 1
NOW
A pure-equity AI × crypto thematic ETF could be filed with the SEC today. The basket of Coinbase, Nvidia, CoreWeave, Marathon, and MicroStrategy represents a fully regulated, exchange-listed portfolio. This is the minimum viable product version of the thesis.
LAYER 2
TODAY
Adding Bitcoin and Ethereum exposure via existing spot ETFs requires no new regulatory approval — these are already SEC-registered products from multiple issuers. A fund-of-funds structure or direct ETF allocation could achieve this today, subject to investment company act considerations.
LAYER 3
FUTURE
Direct inclusion of Bittensor (TAO), Render (RENDER), and Fetch.ai (FET) tokens requires SEC spot ETF approval for each asset — similar to the path Bitcoin and Ethereum traveled. As the regulatory framework matures under the current administration's more crypto-friendly posture, this layer moves from aspirational to achievable.
Data Methodology
Benchmark returns (BTC, SPY, QQQ, BOTZ) are derived from historical closing prices Jan 2024 – Mar 2026, sourced from public market data.  ·  AICR simulated returns are computed from the weighted daily returns of underlying components, fetched live from CoinGecko (crypto) and Yahoo Finance (equities).  ·  Sharpe ratios, volatility, and max drawdown are computed from actual daily returns using standard formulas: annualized volatility = std(daily returns) × √252; Sharpe = (annualized return − 5% risk-free rate) / volatility; max drawdown = largest peak-to-trough decline.  ·  Correlation matrix reflects estimated rolling correlations between asset classes over the study period.  ·  AICR is a hypothetical construct — not a real ETF.
